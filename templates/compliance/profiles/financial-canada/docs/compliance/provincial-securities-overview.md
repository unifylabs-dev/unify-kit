# Provincial Securities Overview (Canada)

> **This is a navigation reference, not legal advice and not regulatory
> claims.** Securities regulation in Canada is fragmented across the
> provinces and territories. The Canadian Securities Administrators
> (CSA) coordinate harmonized rules, but each jurisdiction has its own
> commission and its own registration regime. Your project's
> obligations depend on where your clients are, what activities you
> conduct, and what registration category you hold (or rely on an
> exemption from). Consult registered counsel + your project's
> compliance officer for specifics.

This document is a navigation index — pointers to the right regulator
for the right jurisdiction. It does not author claims about the rules
themselves.

## The Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA)

The CSA is an umbrella organization of provincial and territorial
securities regulators that coordinates pan-Canadian rules through
National Instruments and Multilateral Instruments.

- **CSA homepage**: <https://www.securities-administrators.ca/>
  (accessed 2026-05-12).
- **National Registration Database (NRD)**: where registrants file
  and update registration information. Linked from the CSA site.
- **System for Electronic Disclosure by Insiders (SEDI)** + **SEDAR+**:
  filing systems for public-company disclosures and insider reports.

## Self-regulatory organizations

- **Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization (CIRO)** — combined
  successor to IIROC and the MFDA (effective 2023). Oversees
  investment dealers and mutual fund dealers and their representatives.
  <https://www.ciro.ca/>.
- **OBSI** — Ombudsman for Banking Services and Investments
  (external complaints handling for investment-services firms):
  <https://www.obsi.ca/>.

## Common registration categories (illustrative — not exhaustive)

Your project's registration category drives most of your day-to-day
obligations. Categories vary by province; common ones include:

- Investment Fund Manager.
- Portfolio Manager.
- Investment Dealer (typically also a CIRO member).
- Mutual Fund Dealer.
- Exempt Market Dealer.
- Restricted Dealer (province-specific).
- Crypto Asset Trading Platform (regulated under recent CSA staff
  notices and provincial decisions).

The CSA's National Instrument 31-103 governs registration requirements,
exemptions, and ongoing obligations for most registrant categories. The
companion policy (31-103CP) elaborates.

## How to use this overview

- Identify the jurisdictions where your clients (or investors) are
  located.
- For each jurisdiction, identify the regulator(s) above and any
  CSA national/multilateral instruments applicable to your category.
- Confirm with registered counsel which registration or exemption
  applies, and document the rationale in your project's compliance
  binder.
- Where multiple jurisdictions apply, the CSA's harmonized
  instruments often reduce duplication, but jurisdiction-specific
  amendments and local rules can still apply.
